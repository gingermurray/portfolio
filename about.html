Origin

The Story
So Far

Before storm chasing, storytelling, and content creation, I spent over a decade in Human Resources and customer service — coordinating HR operations, managing HRIS systems, running onboarding and offboarding, keeping organizations compliant, and resolving issues with a people-first mindset. Whether I was helping an employee navigate benefits enrollment or guiding a customer through a complex problem, the throughline was always the same: listen, understand, solve.

That experience gave me a deep respect for systems, processes, and communication — skills that translate directly into leading a 30-chaser operation and building platforms designed around real users.

Growing up in Minnesota, intense weather was just part of life. Towering clouds building on the horizon, the smell of rain rolling in, wind picking up with that unmistakable charge in the air, severe thunderstorm watch boxes flashing across the local news, the wail of tornado sirens cutting through the stillness. I was captivated by all of it. What started as childhood curiosity grew into a deep passion and eventually became my life's calling.

I started chasing seriously in 2022 and joined Tornado Tacklers in 2024 — a network of dedicated storm chasers committed to documenting severe weather and keeping communities informed. I now serve as Team Captain, coordinating chases and managing a media presence that's grown to over 300,000 followers, 183 million views, and 15 million monthly reach. In just a few seasons, I've witnessed 15 tornadoes firsthand — each one a reminder of why this work matters.

I produce all creative assets independently — graphic design, photography, video editing, and live broadcast production — using tools like Canva, Photoshop, DaVinci Resolve, and OBS Studio. From hosting live storm-chasing broadcasts with radar overlays and real-time commentary to designing state-specific severe weather graphics that boosted warning click-through rates by 15%, everything I create is built to inform, engage, and keep communities safe.

On June 20, 2025, I documented multiple tornadoes across North Dakota — from Spiritwood during the day to Fort Ransom after dark. The next morning, I was featured on NewsNation to discuss the outbreak. It was a defining moment that brought everything together: the chasing, the content, the reach.

Along the way, I discovered something unexpected: building the technology behind the content was just as thrilling as the chase itself. I built a custom weather alert system using NWS API data feeds to deliver real-time severe weather warnings to thousands of followers. That led to deeper work with API integrations, live streaming infrastructure, and AI-powered tools — all self-taught by doing, using AI as a collaborator to turn ideas into working code.

Now I'm building Storm Current — a social platform for storm chasers, weather enthusiasts, and followers launching spring 2026 — while continuing to chase, create, and push the Tornado Tacklers brand to new heights.
